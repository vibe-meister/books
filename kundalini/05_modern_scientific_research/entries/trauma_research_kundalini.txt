**TRAUMA RESEARCH AND KUNDALINI**

Modern definition:
Research on trauma and its relationship to the nervous system, body storage of trauma, and healing methods that validates ancient understanding of how trauma blocks energy flow and how kundalini practices can support trauma release and healing. Modern trauma research (particularly polyvagal theory and somatic approaches) corresponds remarkably to ancient descriptions of energy blockages and release.

Historical roots:
Trauma research developed significantly in the late 20th century, with key developments including Bessel van der Kolk's work on trauma storage in the body (1990s), Peter Levine's Somatic Experiencing (1970s–present), and Stephen Porges' polyvagal theory (1990s). This research has revealed how trauma is stored in the nervous system and body, how it affects energy flow, and how body-based practices can support healing—all corresponding to ancient kundalini understanding.

Ancient names & spellings:
- Sanskrit: No direct equivalent (modern: आघात - āghāta, trauma)
- Related concepts:
  - ग्रन्थि (granthi) - knots, blockages (relate to trauma storage)
  - संस्कार (saṃskāra) - impressions, conditioning (relate to trauma patterns)

How it was lost:
The connection between trauma and kundalini wasn't "lost" but rather was never fully made in modern terms because: (1) Ancient practitioners described energy blockages and release without modern trauma vocabulary, (2) Modern trauma research only developed in the late 20th century, (3) The detailed understanding of how kundalini practices support trauma healing was kept in oral transmission and lost when lineages were disrupted, and (4) Integration of trauma understanding with kundalini practices is still developing.

Ties to the book:
Trauma research validates ancient understanding of how energy blockages form and how practices can support release and healing, showing that modern science is catching up to observations made thousands of years ago about the relationship between trauma, the body, and energy flow.
