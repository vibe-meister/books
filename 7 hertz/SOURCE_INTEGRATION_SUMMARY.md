### Source 3: Solfeggio Frequencies - Zebrafish Study (2023)
**Full Citation:**
- Authors: Amanda C Dos Santos, Murilo S de Abreu, Gabriel P de Mello, Vanusa Costella, Nicoli R do Amaral, Alexander Zanella, Júlia Poletto, Elena V Petersen, Allan V Kalueff, Ana C V V Giacomini
- Title: "Solfeggio-frequency music exposure reverses cognitive and endocrine deficits evoked by a 24-h light exposure in adult zebrafish"
- Publication: Behavioral Brain Research
- Year: 2023
- Volume: 450, Article 114461
- DOI: 10.1016/j.bbr.2023.114461
- PMID: 37119977
- URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37119977/
- Access: Paywalled (Elsevier)

**Key Findings Integrated:**
- First rigorous controlled study of Solfeggio frequency effects
- Used zebrafish model with 24h light-induced stress and cognitive deficits
- Solfeggio-frequency music exposure (2h, 6h, or 24h) reversed cognitive deficits
- Normalized elevated cortisol levels (stress hormone)
- Effects seen across different exposure durations
- Authors interpreted as "environmental enrichment" via music, not frequency-specific
- Study did NOT compare Solfeggio frequencies to other music types
- Mechanism unknown; zebrafish translation to humans uncertain

### Source 4: Gao et al. EEG Study (2014)
**Full Citation:**
- Authors: Xiang Gao, Hongbao Cao, Dong Ming, Hongzhi Qi, Xuemin Wang, Xiaolu Wang, Runge Chen, Peng Zhou
- Title: "Analysis of EEG activity in response to binaural beats with different frequencies"
- Publication: International Journal of Psychophysiology
- Year: 2014
- Volume: 94(3), pp. 399-406
- DOI: 10.1016/j.ijpsycho.2014.10.010
- PMID: 25448376
- URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25448376/
- Access: Paywalled (Elsevier)

**Key Findings Integrated:**
- 13 healthy subjects tested with delta (1 Hz), theta (5 Hz), alpha (10 Hz), beta (20 Hz) binaural beats
- Used three measurement methods: Relative Power (RP), Phase Locking Value (PLV), Cross-Mutual Information (CMI)
- **Critical finding: "No clear brainwave entrainment effect was identified"**
- DID observe RP changes and connectivity alterations
- Alternative interpretation: Binaural beats affect functional brain connectivity rather than entrainment
- Important because shows EEG changes WITHOUT entrainment mechanism

**Where Integrated:**
- Binaural beats entry: Moved from "supporting evidence" to "null/contradictory evidence" (top position)
- Emphasized as critical study showing EEG changes without entrainment
- Citations file (full entry with study details)
- Highlights importance of distinguishing connectivity changes from entrainment
