
quirks
(interesting observations and patterns)



players often overvalue what they have and undervalue what they don't

the threat of an action can be more powerful than the action itself

early game mistakes compound, late game mistakes are often recoverable

players tend to optimize locally rather than globally

the "obvious" play is often not the best play

resource hoarding is usually suboptimal

players underestimate the value of flexibility

the player in last place has the most information about what's working

every game has a "critical turn" where the outcome is decided

players often play to not lose rather than to win
