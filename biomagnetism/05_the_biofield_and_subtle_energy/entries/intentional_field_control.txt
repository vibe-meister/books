**INTENTIONAL MAGNETIC FIELD CONTROL**

Modern definition:
The ability to consciously influence one's own magnetic fields through specific practices, thoughts, emotions, and techniques. Research shows that with training, individuals can learn to produce measurable changes in their heart magnetic fields (through HRV) and brain magnetic fields (detectable with MEG), demonstrating that intentional field control is a learnable skill with practical applications for health, performance, and consciousness.

Historical roots:
Intentional field control practices appear in ancient traditions worldwide, though described in different language (energy work, qi cultivation, prana control). These practices were developed through careful observation and experimentation over thousands of years. Modern research beginning in the 1990s has begun to validate these practices by measuring field changes, though the field is still young and much remains to be discovered.

Ancient names & spellings:
- Sanskrit: प्राण नियंत्रण (prāṇa niyaṃtraṇa) - prana control
- Chinese: 氣功 (qìgōng) - energy work, field cultivation
- Related: practices for intentional energy/magnetic field control

How it was lost:
The connection between intentional practices and magnetic field control was lost because: (1) Ancient practitioners described effects in energy terms without understanding magnetic mechanisms, (2) The detailed techniques were kept in oral transmission and lost when lineages were disrupted, (3) Modern science only recently developed instruments to measure these field changes, and (4) The integration of intentional control practices with magnetic field understanding is still developing.

Ties to the book:
Intentional field control represents the practical application of biomagnetism knowledge—the ability to consciously shape one's own magnetic fields for health, performance, and transformation. Understanding that these fields can be intentionally influenced opens possibilities for personal development and optimization.
